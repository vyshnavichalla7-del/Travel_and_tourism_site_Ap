Akka Mahadevi Caves

History

  • Location: Deep inside the Nallamala Hills, on the banks of the Krishna River, near Srisailam, in Kurnool district, Andhra Pradesh
  • Named After: Akkamahadevi, a 12th-century Kannada poet-saint and mystic Akkamahadevi was a Veerashaiva saint and a strong follower of Lord Shiva, especially in the form of Chenna Mallikarjuna (the presiding deity of Srisailam).
  • She renounced worldly life at a young age, lived as a wandering ascetic, and composed Vachanas (short spiritual poems).
  • According to local legend, she stayed in these caves for years of deep meditation, worshipping the Swayambhu (self-manifested) Linga inside the cave.
