Veerabhadra Swamy Temple

History

  • The Rayachoty temple has a history of roughly 1000 years.
  • There are ancient inscriptions: one found near old Rayachoty, in Tamil, dated S.S. 1155 (AD 1233), referring to a king called Nissankapratapa Raya who gave a grant to a temple. Another inscription from the village Abbavaram mentions a king “Thomba” who had his capital at Kalukatayapuram, later moved to Marujavaduyandapuram.
  • Legend: After the incident of Daksha’s Yagnam (a sacrifice) when Lord Shiva created Veerabhadra to avenge the insult to his consort, the fury was later calmed (with Goddess Bhadrakali etc.). It is said that sage Mandavya was involved in calming Veerabhadra, and the place of penance (where Veerabhadra stayed) is Rayachoty.
