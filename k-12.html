Sri Chennakesava Swamy Temple

History

  • Ancient Origins: The temple is believed to have been constructed approximately 1,200 years ago, during the Chola or early Vijayanagara period. The main deity, Sri Chennakesava Swamy, is depicted with four arms holding the Shanku (conch), Chakra (discus), Gada (mace), and Abhaya (blessing). The idol faces north, accompanied by Sridevi and Bhudevi on either side.
  • Spiritual Significance: The temple holds profound significance in the Vaishnavite tradition, particularly for devotees of Lord Vishnu. It is renowned for its association with Annamacharya, the 15th-century saint-poet who composed over 32,000 devotional hymns dedicated to Lord Venkateswara. Annamacharya's family was deeply devoted to Chennakesava Swamy, and local legends suggest that the deity blessed Annamacharya by inscribing sacred syllables on his tongue, enabling him to compose his hymns.
