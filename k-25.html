Pala Kondalu Waterfalls

History

  • Palakondalu is a natural waterfall area located near Puttampalli village, close to Kadapa, Andhra Pradesh. It lies in a hilly, forested terrain as part of what are called the Palakonda (or “Kadapa Palakonda”) hills.
  • There is an ancient temple at or near the site, dedicated to Lord Mahavishnu (referred to locally as Palakondarayana Swamy) along with a Goddess Devi. The temple is believed to date from the 11th century.
  • Besides the main waterfall, there are multiple smaller ones (subsidiary waterfall streams) around the same area. The larger fall is the main attraction, but visitors can see / access smaller falls nearby.
  • The surroundings are densely forested (partly Nallamala forest region) and the terrain involves walking / trekking (through some forest patches, hill paths, steps) to reach viewpoints / the falls.
  • The area is becoming more well-known / promoted for tourism. Local administration has noted that Palakondalu is among the natural attractions around Kadapa, along with others.
