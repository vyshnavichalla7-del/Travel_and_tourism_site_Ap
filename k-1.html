Sri Kodandarama Swamy Temple

History

  • Origins: The temple was established by the Vijayanagara kings, with a local legend attributing its creation to the Nishada (Boya) community leaders Vontudu and Mittudu, who became devoted followers of Rama and were later transformed into stone.
  • Significance: The temple is famed for being where the saint-poet Bammera Potana wrote his magnum opus, Maha Bhagavatham, in the Telugu language.
  • Architecture: The temple is notable for its large size and Vijayanagara style of architecture, featuring multiple ornate gopurams (towers) and an intricately carved mandapa.
  • Notable Visitors: Famous figures like the saint-poet Annamacharya and the French traveler Jean-Baptiste Tavernier visited the temple.
