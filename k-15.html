Mandem Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple

History

  • The temple is located in Devunipalli village, Kadapa district.
  • Legend says that once a large rock fell from the nearby hill and stopped near a slope without injuring houses. The rock split into two, and from that stone form the Swamy (deity) emerged.
  • The next day, the deity appeared in a villager’s dream and asked for a temple to be constructed in his honor. On the hill, Swamy’s names and footprints were found.
  • It is said that the temple was built by Jains originally.
  • A distinctive feature: the temple faces west (unusual, since most Hindu temples are east-facing).
