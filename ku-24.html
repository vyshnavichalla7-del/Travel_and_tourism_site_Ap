Paladara Panchadara

History

The name Paladara Panchadara comes from two Sanskrit-Telugu words:

  • "Pala" means milk,
  • "Dara" means stream or flow,
  • "Pancha" means five,
  • "Dara" again refers to streams.
  • Together, it refers to a place where five sacred streams of milky-white water emerge from the rocks.
  • According to Hindu mythology, this is the spot where Lord Narasimha washed his hands and face after killing the demon Hiranyakashipu.
  • The water is said to have emerged from the mountains by the grace of Lord Narasimha, and it is considered extremely pure and holy. It flows down from the hills continuously throughout the year.
  • Sri Adi Shankaracharya, during his travels to Ahobilam, is believed to have composed the "Lakshmi Narasimha Karavalambam Stotram" at this very place, inspired by the divinity and natural beauty of the site
