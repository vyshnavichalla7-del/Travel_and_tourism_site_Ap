Vijayadurgadevi Temple

History

  • The temple is believed to be several centuries old, with roots in the medieval period, though the exact date of construction is unclear.
  • It is said to have been patronized by local Palegars (feudal chiefs) or rulers who ruled the Rayalaseema region, especially during the Vijayanagara Empire (14th to 17th centuries).
  • The architectural style suggests Dravidian influence, common in temples built during the Vijayanagara period.
