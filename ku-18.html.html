Sri Chenchu Lakshmi Tribal Museum

History

  • The museum is named after Chenchu Lakshmi, who, according to local legend, was a Chenchu tribal woman believed to be an incarnation of Goddess Parvati.
  • Mythology says that Lord Shiva took the form of Mallikarjuna and married Chenchu Lakshmi in the forests of Srisailam, symbolizing the union of divinity with the tribal spirit of nature.
  • This story plays a major role in the cultural identity of the Chenchu people and their connection to the sacred hills of Srisailam
