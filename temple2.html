History

The Shri Varaha Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple at Simhachalam is believed to have origins dating back to the Krita (Satya) Yuga, originally constructed by Prahlada, the devotee of Lord Vishnu. Legend says Lord Vishnu appeared here as Narasimha, the half-man half-lion avatar, to protect Prahlada by killing the demon Hiranyakashipu. Over centuries, the temple gained patronage from the Cholas, Eastern Ganga dynasty (notably Narasimhadeva I in the 13th century), and the Vijayanagara Empire.
