1NS kursura submarine museum

Overview<

The INS Kurusura Submarine Museum in Visakhapatnam is Asia's first submarine turned museum and the second of its kind in the world. It is located on the golden sands of Ramakrishna Beach (RK Beach) in Visakhapatnam. Key Details: The submarine, INS Kurusura, is a Soviet-built Kalvari-class submarine commissioned in 1969 and decommissioned in 2001 after 31 years of service in the Indian Navy. It played a significant role including during the 1971 Indo-Pak war. The entire submarine, along with its weapon systems, was brought ashore and placed on a concrete foundation to create the museum. The museum showcases interiors such as the control room, radar room, sonar room, crew living quarters, kitchen, and dining areas with realistic human-sized models. The museum is a unique maritime heritage landmark, displaying a blend of historical, technical, and experiential insights about submarine life and naval defense
