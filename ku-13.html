Hatakeswaram Temple

History

The Hatakeswaram Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is situated approximately 5 km from the main Srisailam temple, nestled on the foothills of the Nallamala Hills. The temple's name is derived from two legends:

  • The Potter's Devotion: According to one legend, a potter, an ardent devotee of Lord Shiva, once found himself without food to offer to a visiting pilgrim. In his distress, he prayed to Lord Shiva. Pleased with his devotion, Lord Shiva appeared in the form of a pot and provided food, leading to the temple's name "Atikeswaram," which later evolved to "Hatakeswaram."
  • Association with Hatha Yoga: Another interpretation links the temple's name to the yogic tradition of Hatha Yoga. Some believe the temple was a center for Hatha Yoga practices, contributing to its name.
    • The current stone structure of the temple is believed to have been constructed around the 12th century
