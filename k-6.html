Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple

History

  • The presiding deity is Lord Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy. He is in the form called Ashta Bahu Narasimha (eight arms), depicted tearing the demon Hiranyakashipu. Beside Him stands Prahlada with folded hands.
  • The idol is believed to be svayambhu (self‑emanated) from the roots of a “Khadri” tree. “Khadri” or “Kadiri” possibly refers to a mulberry tree (or similar local tree).
  • Legend holds that after killing Hiranyakashipu, Lord Narasimha was still filled with rage and roamed in forests. Devas and Prahlada prayed for him to calm down. He appeared here, emerged from the tree roots, and calmed His anger. That makes this location very sacred.
  • The name Kadiri is said to derive from “Ka” = Vishnu’s foot / padam, and “Adri” = hill, or from “Kadri tree.” Over time Kadiri / Khadri.
  • Historically, the temple has contributions from rulers in the Vijayanagara dynasty. For example, inscriptions say that Veera Bukka Raya, Achyutha Devaraya, Thirumala Raya etc., made additions (corridors, gopurams, mantapas etc.).
