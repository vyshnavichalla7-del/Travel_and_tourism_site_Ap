kailasagiri hill park

Overview<

Kailasagiri Hill Park in Visakhapatnam is a scenic hilltop park spread over 100 acres, perched at an altitude of about 360 feet, offering panoramic views of the Bay of Bengal and the city below. It is one of the most popular tourist spots in Vizag, known for its lush greenery, serene atmosphere, and multiple attractions. Key Features: Statues: The park is famous for the impressive 40-feet tall white statues of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati embracing, symbolizing divine love and tranquility. Floral Clock: One of the largest floral clocks in India, with a diameter of around 10 feet, located near the statues. Ropeway: The hilltop is accessible via a scenic ropeway ride, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Other Attractions: Includes Titanic viewpoint, Jungle trails, Shanti Ashram, Shanku Chakra Nama (symbolizing Lord Vishnu), a toy train, and facilities for paragliding and horse riding. Atmosphere: Known for its fresh breeze, greenery, and spectacular views of the illuminated city and sea, especially mesmerizing during evenings. Accessibility: Easily reachable by road, the park has well-developed pathways and pedestrian stairways. Eco-Friendly: The hill park is a designated plastic-free zone, promoting environmental sustainability. Kailasagiri combines natural beauty, cultural symbolism, and recreational activities, making it a must-visit destination for tourists in Visakhapatnam.
