Sri Bugga Ramalingeswara Temple

History

  • Constructed between 1490 and 1509 during the reign of the Vijayanagara Empire, the temple was commissioned by Pemmasani Ramalinga Nayudu I, a chieftain of the Gutti-Gandikota region. According to historical records, the temple was built by Ramalinga Nayudu, a chieftain of the Vijayanagara Kings in the 16th century. The temple reflects beautiful and marvelous architecture, with a completion period spanning 21 years.
  • The temple's name, "Bugga," derives from a perennial underground stream that continuously flows into the sanctum sanctorum, where the naturally occurring (swayambhu) Shiva linga is consecrated. This unique feature has made the temple a subject of architectural and spiritual interest.
  • Architecturally, the temple exhibits a blend of Chalukyan, Vijayanagara, and Chola styles. Notably, the temple's north gopuram, although unfinished, showcases intricate carvings and sculptures, including depictions from the Ramayana and Mahabharata. A miniature stone chariot resembling the one at the Vittala Temple in Hampi is also present.
